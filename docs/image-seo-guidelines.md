# Editorial Image SEO Guidelines

A Drop of Seoul uses images as editorial context, not decoration. Every article image should help a reader understand the story before they read the headline.

## Selection Rules

- Use one primary image per article and avoid reusing the same visual across articles in the same series.
- Match the subject: sunscreen stories should show SPF or sun behavior, clinic stories should show a treatment context, routine stories should show the relevant ritual.
- Prefer unbranded or lightly branded images. Do not make a stock model or visible product look like an endorsement.
- Use free-use sources with clear licenses, and keep the original source URL in code or documentation.
- Keep the tone warm, bright, editorial, and practical. Avoid overly glossy product-ad compositions unless the article is explicitly a product guide.

## File Rules

- Save local article images under `public/images/articles/`.
- Use descriptive kebab-case filenames that include the topic, for example `korean-sunscreen-as-skincare.jpg`.
- Prepare images at roughly 1600px on the long edge for hero/thumbnail reuse.
- Use JPG for photography and keep file size reasonable before committing.

## Alt Text Rules

- Write alt text for what is visible, not a keyword list.
- Mention the article concept only when it is genuinely visible or useful: "woman applying sunscreen on her arm" is better than "best Korean sunscreen SPF skincare K-beauty".
- Keep alt text concise, usually under 125 characters.
- Do not start with "image of" or "photo of" unless the medium matters.
- Do not describe race, age, or gender unless it is relevant to the story.

## Caption And Credit Rules

- Use captions to connect the image to the article's idea in one useful sentence.
- Credit the photographer/source when a platform recommends it, even if not required.
- Link credits to the source page and license page when available.
- Captions should not repeat the headline. They should add context.

## SEO / AI Search Rules

- Use the same image consistently for card, article hero, Open Graph, and social previews.
- Pair every image with visible surrounding text: headline, caption, and relevant paragraph context.
- Keep the image path descriptive and stable; do not rename after indexing unless necessary.
- Use structured, people-first article text around the image. There is no separate "AI search image trick" beyond clarity, relevance, accessibility, and crawlable context.
