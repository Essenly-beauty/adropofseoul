Korean serum shelves are crowded for a reason — dozens of formulas chase the same handful of concerns, and most of them are genuinely good. We narrowed a crowded field down to five bottles worth repurchasing by skin type, plus one that the internet swears by that we'd steer you away from.

Korean serums are not interchangeable. A snail-mucin formula that fixes a dehydrated winter face will sit on oily skin like a raincoat. So instead of one "best of" list, here's what to reach for by skin type, with the actual texture, price, and who should walk past it.

> **How we evaluated these**
>
> - Each serum assessed solo for at least two weeks, layered into the same basic routine (cleanse, serum, moisturizer, SPF), so results weren't muddied by other actives.
> - Priced from **Olive Young** and **Amazon** as of publication — prices shift with seasonal sales, so treat these as a range rather than a fixed number. Olive Young is usually cheaper if you can order internationally.
> - Judged on absorption, next-day skin feel, and reported breakouts or stinging based on ingredient profiles and widely shared user feedback.
> - Not derm advice — patch test anything new, especially with diagnosed eczema, rosacea, or pregnancy. These are editorial picks based on formulation, brand track record, and publicly available user feedback, not clinical trials.

## Best for oily skin: Anua Heartleaf 77% Niacinamide Serum

Oily skin doesn't need less hydration — it needs hydration that doesn't sit on top like a film. Anua's serum is thin, almost watery, and sinks in within a minute: **10%** niacinamide plus heartleaf extract to calm the redness that oily, breakout-prone skin often carries alongside the shine. It's one of the more consistently well-reviewed niacinamide serums at this price point, with users frequently citing it as gentle enough for daily use even alongside other actives like retinoids or vitamin C.

**Price:** around **$17–19** for 30ml on Amazon; often cheaper on Olive Young (roughly ₩17,000–20,000 in Korea before any app discount).

**Skip if:** you're already using a strong niacinamide elsewhere — layering two can cause the flushing some users report above 10%.

## Best for dry skin: Torriden Dive-In Low Molecule Hyaluronic Acid Serum

This is the one we recommend first when someone says their skin feels "tight by 2pm." Torriden uses five molecular weights of hyaluronic acid, so it hydrates at the surface and slightly deeper, instead of sitting on top the way single-weight HA serums can. It's become one of the most recommended entry-level hydration serums in K-beauty circles, prized for a lightweight, non-tacky finish that layers well under heavier creams.

**Price:** roughly **$16** for 50ml — one of the better cost-per-ml serums here (around ₩20,000 at Olive Young Korea, frequently discounted further during sales).

**Skip if:** you live somewhere very dry or run a lot of AC/heating — HA alone can pull moisture from your skin if nothing seals it in, so pair it with a real cream.

## Best for combination skin: Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum (Propolis + Niacinamide)

The internet's most-hyped Korean serum earns it here, specifically for combination skin — the propolis brings softness for drier cheeks while the niacinamide keeps the T-zone in check. It's the one serum on this list we'd call genuinely balanced rather than built for an extreme, and it's also one of the most reviewed Korean serums on both Amazon and Olive Young Global — a large enough track record that its reputation for gentle, everyday brightening looks like more than hype.

**Price:** about **$17** for 30ml, often under $14 during Olive Young sales (around ₩13,000–15,000 in Korea, where it's a near-permanent bestseller).

**Skip if:** you need fragrance-free — it has a mild honey scent from the propolis.

## Best for sensitive skin: Isntree Hyaluronic Acid Serum

For sensitive or reactive skin, fewer ingredients doing one obvious job wins. Isntree's version skips fragrance, essential oils, and alcohol high on the ingredient list, leaning on plain hydration and a touch of panthenol instead of anything that brightens or exfoliates. It's frequently recommended in sensitive-skin and rosacea-adjacent skincare communities specifically because it rarely triggers reactions, even among users who react to most actives.

**Price:** typically **$15–18** for 50ml (roughly ₩15,000–17,000 at Olive Young Korea).

**Skip if:** you want active exfoliation or brightening too — this category is deliberately boring, which is the point for reactive skin.

## Best for acne-prone skin: Some By Mi AHA-BHA-PHA 30 Days Miracle Serum

This has the most extreme before/afters online, and the reputation is earned — for the right skin. The tea tree, niacinamide, and mixed-acid blend is genuinely strong, which is also why it's most likely to cause purging or irritation in the first two weeks if you go nightly. Reviews split cleanly along skin-type lines: consistently high praise from oily, acne-prone users, and consistently reported irritation from anyone with a compromised or reactive barrier.

**Price:** around **$20–24** for 50ml (about ₩20,000–23,000 at Olive Young Korea).

**Skip if:** you have active eczema or a compromised barrier — start at **2–3 nights a week**, and always follow with SPF the next morning.

## The one we'd skip: Numbuzin No.5 Vitamin Niacinamide Serum

We wanted to like this one more than we do. It has a devoted following for "instant glow," and the marketing is excellent. But the glow it delivers reads more like a temporary silicone-adjacent finish than an actual improvement — the kind of result that photographs well and fades by lunch. That gap between social-media reputation and longer-term reviews shows up consistently once the initial hype window passes.

**Who it's for:** a one-night-event glow before a party. **Who should skip it:** anyone hoping for the long-term brightening that Beauty of Joseon or Anua deliver more consistently.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between a Korean serum and a Western one?**
Mostly philosophy — Korean serums tend to be lighter and built around one or two hero ingredients (niacinamide, centella, hyaluronic acid) rather than heavy multi-active blends, making them easier to mix and match by concern.

**Can I use more than one serum from this list at once?**
Usually, if you layer thin-to-thick — but don't stack two high-strength actives (a niacinamide serum and an AHA-BHA-PHA serum) without easing in slowly. Introduce one new active at a time, on alternating nights, and watch for redness or peeling before combining them into a nightly routine.

**Where's the cheapest place to buy these — Amazon or Olive Young?**
Olive Young Global usually beats Amazon on price, especially during seasonal sales; Amazon ships faster in the US with easier returns. Factor in shipping time either way — Olive Young Global orders to the US typically take one to two weeks.

**How long before a serum shows results?**
Hydration serums (HA, centella) show a difference in days. Brightening and acid-based serums need **4–6 weeks** of consistent use — most people quit right before that point.
