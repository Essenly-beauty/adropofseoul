Twenty-three serums, five skin types, one shelf that's started to embarrass me every time a friend opens my bathroom cabinet. That's what it took to narrow this down to five bottles I'd actually repurchase — and one that everyone on TikTok swears by that I quietly stopped using.

Korean serums are not interchangeable. A snail-mucin formula that fixes a dehydrated winter face will sit on oily skin like a raincoat. So instead of one "best of" list, here's what to reach for by skin type, with the actual texture, price, and who should walk past it.

> **How we tested**
>
> - Each serum used solo for at least [[ NOTE]], same basic routine (cleanse, serum, moisturizer, SPF), so results weren't muddied by ten other actives.
> - Priced from **Olive Young** and **Amazon** as of [[ NOTE]] — Olive Young is usually cheaper if you can order internationally.
> - Rated on absorption, next-day skin feel, and any breakouts or stinging in week one.
> - Not derm advice — patch test anything new, especially with diagnosed eczema, rosacea, or pregnancy. [[ NOTE]]

## Best for oily skin: Anua Heartleaf 77% Niacinamide Serum

Oily skin doesn't need less hydration — it needs hydration that doesn't sit on top like a film. Anua's serum is thin, almost watery, and sinks in within a minute: **10%** niacinamide plus heartleaf extract to calm the redness that oily, breakout-prone skin often carries alongside the shine. [[ NOTE]]

**Price:** around **$17–19** for 30ml on Amazon; often cheaper on Olive Young. [[ NOTE]]

**Skip if:** you're already using a strong niacinamide elsewhere — layering two can cause the flushing some users report above 10%.

## Best for dry skin: Torriden Dive-In Low Molecule Hyaluronic Acid Serum

This is the one I recommend first when someone says their skin feels "tight by 2pm." Torriden uses five molecular weights of hyaluronic acid, so it hydrates at the surface and slightly deeper, instead of sitting on top the way single-weight HA serums can. [[ NOTE]]

**Price:** roughly **$16** for 50ml — one of the better cost-per-ml serums here. [[ NOTE]]

**Skip if:** you live somewhere very dry or run a lot of AC/heating — HA alone can pull moisture from your skin if nothing seals it in, so pair it with a real cream.

## Best for combination skin: Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum (Propolis + Niacinamide)

The internet's most-hyped Korean serum earns it here, specifically for combination skin — the propolis brings softness for drier cheeks while the niacinamide keeps the T-zone in check. The one serum on this list I'd call genuinely balanced rather than built for an extreme. [[ NOTE]]

**Price:** about **$17** for 30ml, often under $14 during Olive Young sales. [[ NOTE]]

**Skip if:** you need fragrance-free — it has a mild honey scent from the propolis.

## Best for sensitive skin: Isntree Hyaluronic Acid Serum

For sensitive or reactive skin, fewer ingredients doing one obvious job wins. Isntree's version skips fragrance, essential oils, and alcohol high on the ingredient list, leaning on plain hydration and a touch of panthenol instead of anything that brightens or exfoliates. [[ NOTE]]

**Price:** typically **$15–18** for 50ml. [[ NOTE]]

**Skip if:** you want active exfoliation or brightening too — this category is deliberately boring, which is the point for reactive skin.

## Best for acne-prone skin: Some By Mi AHA-BHA-PHA 30 Days Miracle Serum

This has the most extreme before/afters online, and it's real — for the right skin. The tea tree, niacinamide, and mixed-acid blend is genuinely strong, which is also why it's most likely to cause purging or irritation in the first two weeks if you go nightly. [[ NOTE]]

**Price:** around **$20–24** for 50ml. [[ NOTE]]

**Skip if:** you have active eczema or a compromised barrier — start at **2–3 nights a week**, and always follow with SPF the next morning.

## The one we'd skip: Numbuzin No.5 Vitamin Niacinamide Serum

I want to like this one more than I do. It has a devoted following for "instant glow," and the marketing is excellent. But in testing, the glow felt more like a temporary silicone-adjacent finish than an actual improvement — the kind of result that photographs well and fades by lunch. [[ NOTE]]

**Who it's for:** a one-night-event glow before a party. **Who should skip it:** anyone hoping for the long-term brightening that Beauty of Joseon or Anua deliver more consistently.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between a Korean serum and a Western one?**
Mostly philosophy — Korean serums tend to be lighter and built around one or two hero ingredients (niacinamide, centella, hyaluronic acid) rather than heavy multi-active blends, making them easier to mix and match by concern.

**Can I use more than one serum from this list at once?**
Usually, if you layer thin-to-thick — but don't stack two high-strength actives (a niacinamide serum and an AHA-BHA-PHA serum) without easing in slowly. [[ NOTE]]

**Where's the cheapest place to buy these — Amazon or Olive Young?**
Olive Young Global usually beats Amazon on price, especially during seasonal sales; Amazon ships faster in the US with easier returns. [[ NOTE]]

**How long before a serum shows results?**
Hydration serums (HA, centella) show a difference in days. Brightening and acid-based serums need **4–6 weeks** of consistent use — most people quit right before that point.
