## What it is

Snail mucin — labeled snail secretion filtrate on ingredient lists — is the
trail snails leave behind as they move, collected and processed for use in
skincare. It's a mix of glycoproteins, hyaluronic acid, and small amounts of
allantoin and glycolic acid, which together give it a distinctive slippery,
tacky texture. It became a staple of Korean essences and creams because it
hydrates without feeling greasy and layers easily under other steps.

## What it does

The glycoproteins and hyaluronic acid in snail mucin draw water into the
skin's surface, leaving it noticeably plumper and softer after use. Many
people reach for it after using a retinoid or exfoliating acid, since it
seems to help redness and flaking settle down faster. With regular use it
can also smooth out rough patches and give an overall more even texture,
making it a common pick for skin that's dry, reactive, or recovering from
a damaged barrier.

## How to use it

Apply as an essence or serum after cleansing and toning, before heavier
creams. It's gentle enough for both morning and night, and works well
sandwiched between a hydrating toner and a moisturizer on days when skin
feels tight or freshly exfoliated.
