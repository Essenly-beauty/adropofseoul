## What it is

Centella asiatica is a small, leafy herb — also known as cica, gotu kola, or
tiger grass — that has been used in traditional medicine for wound healing
for centuries. In skincare it's extracted for its active compounds,
including madecassoside and asiaticoside, and became a Korean skincare
mainstay through "cica" creams and ampoules aimed at calming irritated or
compromised skin.

## What it does

Centella's active compounds help visibly reduce redness and support the
skin's natural barrier repair process, which is why it's a go-to for skin
that's reactive, breakout-prone, or recovering from over-exfoliation or
sun exposure. It also carries antioxidant properties that help protect
skin from everyday environmental stress. Because it calms without heavy
occlusive ingredients, it's a common pairing for acne-prone skin that
still needs soothing support.

## How to use it

Look for it in toners, ampoules, and creams layered right after cleansing,
especially on days when skin feels flushed, tight, or freshly treated with
an active ingredient. It's gentle enough for daily morning and night use
and pairs well with barrier-supporting moisturizers.
